# Victoria Flooring Outlet — Brand Identity Guidelines

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** March 12, 2026  
**Market:** Victoria, BC & Vancouver Island

## 1. Brand Essence

### Brand Promise
**"Honest pricing. Local trust. Quality floors."**

Victoria Flooring Outlet delivers premium flooring products at outlet prices, with no markup deception and genuine expertise from a locally-owned Victoria business.

### Brand Personality
- **Honest & Transparent** — No-fluff communication, straightforward pricing
- **Local & Trustworthy** — Victoria roots, community-focused
- **Knowledgeable & Helpful** — Expert guidance without pressure
- **Quality-Conscious** — Premium products at fair prices

### Brand Voice Attributes
- **Tone:** Conversational, confident, reassuring
- **Style:** Direct but warm, professional without being corporate
- **Approach:** Educational first, sales second
- **Language:** Plain-spoken, avoiding jargon unless explaining technical specs

## 2. Visual Identity

### Color Palette

#### Primary Colors
```
Charcoal       #2B2B2B    — Primary text, headers, navigation
Charcoal Dark  #1A1A1A    — Strong emphasis, footer backgrounds
Sand           #C8B49A    — Accent color, warm highlights
Gold           #D4A017    — CTAs, trust badges, key highlights
```

#### Secondary Colors
```
Sand Light     #F0E9E0    — Section backgrounds, light panels
Sand Pale      #FAF7F4    — Page backgrounds, subtle zones
Gold Light     #E8B824    — Hover states, active elements
Gold Pale      #FFF8E7    — Alert backgrounds, promotional banners
Grey           #8C8C8C    — Supporting text, captions
Grey Light     #DCDCDC    — Borders, dividers
White          #FFFFFF    — Main background, card surfaces
```

#### Color Psychology & Usage
- **Charcoal:** Authority, quality, sophistication (not cold or corporate)
- **Sand:** Natural materials, warmth, home comfort, oak flooring tones
- **Gold:** Value, premium quality, trust badges, special offers
- **White/Sand Pale:** Clean spaces, light-filled rooms (aspirational feel)

### Typography

#### Font Families
- **Headings:** Montserrat (weights: 400, 600, 700, 800, 900)
- **Body Text:** Open Sans (weights: 400, 600, italic 400)
- **Fallbacks:** system-ui, -apple-system, sans-serif

#### Type Scale
```
H1: clamp(2rem, 5vw, 3.5rem)     — Hero headlines
H2: clamp(1.6rem, 3.5vw, 2.5rem) — Section headers
H3: clamp(1.2rem, 2.5vw, 1.6rem) — Subsection headers
H4: 1.1rem (weight: 700)          — Card titles, small headers
Body: 17px / 1.7 line-height      — Paragraph text
```

#### Typography Rules
- **Line Height:** 1.2 for headings, 1.7 for body text
- **Letter Spacing:** Default (no custom tracking)
- **Font Weight Hierarchy:**
  - 800-900: Hero headlines, major CTAs
  - 700: Subheads, emphasis, buttons
  - 600: Section labels, minor emphasis
  - 400: Body copy, navigation

### Design Elements

#### Shadows
```
Small:  0 2px 8px rgba(0,0,0,0.08)   — Cards, small elevations
Medium: 0 4px 20px rgba(0,0,0,0.12)  — Modals, featured cards
Large:  0 8px 40px rgba(0,0,0,0.18)  — Hero sections, key CTAs
```

#### Border Radius
```
Small:  6px   — Buttons, tags, small UI elements
Medium: 12px  — Cards, panels, images
Large:  20px  — Hero sections, feature boxes
```

#### Spacing System
- **Section Padding:** 80px (desktop), scales down on mobile
- **Container Max Width:** 1140px
- **Grid Gaps:** 24px (mobile), 32px (tablet), 40px (desktop)

## 4. Visual Direction

### Photography Style

#### Product Photography
- **Lighting:** Bright, natural light (not harsh studio lighting)
- **Context:** Installed in real rooms, not isolated on white
- **Angles:** Slight perspective (not perfectly flat overhead)
- **Focus:** Sharp grain detail, true-to-life color

#### Lifestyle Photography
- **Rooms:** Bright, airy, aspirational but achievable
- **Style:** Modern transitional (not ultra-modern or traditional)
- **People:** Real families, natural poses (avoid stock photo stiffness)
- **Mood:** Comfortable, lived-in, warm, inviting

#### Color Treatment
- Warm color grading (slightly golden/sandy tones)
- High brightness, moderate contrast
- Avoid cold blue or harsh white balance
- Match sand palette undertones

### Layout Principles

#### Page Structure
- **Hero Sections:** Large, impactful, clear value proposition
- **White Space:** Generous (don't crowd content)
- **Visual Hierarchy:** Clear flow from headline → benefits → CTA
- **Scanning Patterns:** F-pattern and Z-pattern friendly

#### Grid System
- **Columns:** 12-column responsive grid
- **Breakpoints:**
  - Desktop: 1140px max-width container
  - Tablet: 1000px breakpoint
  - Mobile: 700px breakpoint
- **Gaps:** 24-40px depending on screen size

#### Card Design
- Rounded corners (12px radius)
- Subtle shadow (medium elevation)
- Sand-pale or white background
- Clear hierarchy (image → title → description → CTA)

## 5. Content Guidelines

### Writing Style

#### Headlines
- **Format:** Benefit-driven or question-based
- **Length:** 6-12 words ideal
- **Tone:** Confident but not boastful
- **Examples:**
  - ✅ "The Best Flooring Deal in Victoria? Quiet, Durable, and Honestly Priced."
  - ✅ "Why This Week's Floor Is a Smart Investment"
  - ❌ "AMAZING SALE!!! DON'T MISS OUT!!!"
  - ❌ "Harbinger Signature Acoustic Click Product Page"

#### Body Copy
- **Sentence Length:** Vary between short and medium (avoid long complex sentences)
- **Paragraph Length:** 2-4 sentences max
- **Formatting:** Use bullets, subheads, bold for scannability
- **Tone Examples:**
  - ✅ "This floor won't buckle under pressure—literally."
  - ✅ "Let's talk about what makes this deal special."
  - ❌ "Our innovative flooring solutions leverage cutting-edge technology..."
  - ❌ "Please contact us for more information regarding..."

#### Calls to Action
- **Primary CTAs:** Direct, benefit-focused
  - "See the Deal & Get Your Sample"
  - "Order Your Free Sample Today"
  - "Get a Custom Quote"
- **Secondary CTAs:** Exploratory, low-pressure
  - "Browse Similar Floors"
  - "Read the Full Specs"
  - "Learn More About LVP"

## 6. Competitive Differentiation

### Market Position
**"The honest alternative to big-box flooring stores"**

### Key Differentiators

#### 1. Pricing Transparency
**Us:** Outlet pricing model, no inflated "compare-at" prices  
**Them:** Markup pricing with constant "sales"

#### 2. Local Expertise
**Us:** Victoria-based, understands local homes and styles  
**Them:** National chains with generic product knowledge

#### 3. Curated Selection
**Us:** Hand-picked quality products, deep knowledge of each  
**Them:** Overwhelming SKU count, minimal guidance

#### 4. No-Pressure Approach
**Us:** Educational content, help customers choose right product  
**Them:** Commission-driven sales tactics

### Competitive Set

#### Direct Competitors
- Home Depot Victoria (big-box, overwhelming selection)
- Lowe's Victoria (similar to Home Depot)
- Local flooring retailers (higher pricing, showroom focus)

#### Indirect Competitors
- Online flooring retailers (shipping costs, can't see/touch product)
- General contractors (markup on materials)

### Messaging Against Competition

**When compared to big-box stores:**
→ "Expert guidance instead of aisle-wandering confusion"  
→ "Honest outlet pricing, not fake sale events"

**When compared to high-end showrooms:**
→ "Same quality brands, outlet prices"  
→ "No showroom overhead passed on to you"

**When compared to online retailers:**
→ "See and touch before you buy"  
→ "Local pickup or Island delivery—no surprise shipping costs"

## 8. Brand Do's and Don'ts

### DO:
✅ Use plain language and clear explanations  
✅ Show real product installations in bright, inviting spaces  
✅ Emphasize honest pricing and local roots  
✅ Provide detailed specs for informed decisions  
✅ Offer genuine help (not sales pressure)  
✅ Use warm, natural color grading in photography  
✅ Give space for content to breathe (white space is good)  

### DON'T:
❌ Use fake urgency tactics ("ONLY 3 LEFT!!!")  
❌ Manipulate "compare-at" pricing  
❌ Use stock photos that look obviously staged  
❌ Overcomplicate product descriptions with jargon  
❌ Pressure visitors into immediate purchase  
❌ Use cold, corporate language  
❌ Clutter layouts with too many elements
