## Product Messaging Templates

### Deal of the Week

**Headline Formula:**
`[Product Feature] + [Customer Benefit] + [Price Hook]`

**Examples:**
- "Quiet Comfort Meets Durable Style—and It's On Sale"
- "Commercial-Grade Wear Layer, Residential Price"
- "The Floor That Handles Everything Your Family Throws at It (14% Off)"

**Body Structure:**
1. **Hook:** What makes this deal special (feature + benefit)
2. **Details:** Technical specs that matter (wear layer, sound control, etc.)
3. **Value:** Why this price is significant (compare-at, percentage off)
4. **Call to Action:** Get sample, see details, or buy now

**Example:**
> This week's deal brings you something special: the Harbinger Signature Acoustic Click with an integrated cork underlayment. What does that mean for you? A noticeably quieter home. The kind of floor that absorbs sound from footsteps, dropped toys, and everyday life—while standing up to all of it with a commercial-grade 0.55mm wear layer. It's $4.29/sqft this week (regularly $4.99), and it's the smart choice for anyone upgrading main living areas.

### Product Category Pages

**Vinyl Plank (LVP):**
> "Vinyl plank flooring gives you the look of hardwood with the durability of, well, vinyl. Waterproof, scratch-resistant, and easy to install—perfect for kitchens, bathrooms, and busy family areas."

**Laminate:**
> "Laminate flooring delivers the classic look of wood at an accessible price point. Durable, DIY-friendly, and available in a range of styles—from modern oak to rustic reclaimed looks."

**Hardwood:**
> "Real hardwood never goes out of style. It ages beautifully, adds value to your home, and brings a warmth that synthetic materials can't match. We carry solid and engineered options."

**Tile (if applicable):**
> "Tile is the ultimate in durability and versatility. From ceramic to porcelain, for floors or walls—tile delivers timeless style that lasts decades."

## Objection Handling

### "I can get it cheaper online"

**Response:**
> "Maybe—but can you see it, touch it, and verify the quality before buying? Can you pick it up today or get free Island delivery? Can you talk to someone who actually knows the product? Online has its place, but for flooring, we think seeing and touching matters."

**Key Points:**
- Shipping costs add up (especially for heavy flooring)
- No surprises when you see product in person
- Local support if something goes wrong

### "Home Depot has more options"

**Response:**
> "They do—over 10,000 SKUs in some stores. But more isn't always better. We carry a curated selection of quality products we actually know inside out. Instead of wandering aisles hoping to find the right floor, you'll talk to someone who can guide you to it."

**Key Points:**
- Curated > overwhelming
- Expert knowledge > aisle wandering
- We know what we sell (they stock everything)

### "How do I know this 'sale' is real?"

**Response:**
> "Fair question—a lot of flooring stores inflate prices then run constant 'sales.' We don't. Our outlet pricing is straightforward: direct from distributors, with minimal markup, every day. When we run a Deal of the Week, it's a genuine discount on already-fair pricing."

**Key Points:**
- Outlet model = everyday low prices
- No inflated "compare-at" pricing
- Transparent about margins

### "I'm not sure which product I need"

**Response:**
> "That's exactly why we're here. Tell us about the room (kitchen? bedroom? basement?), how it gets used (kids? pets? high traffic?), and what look you're going for. We'll match you to the right product—not the most expensive one, the right one."

**Key Points:**
- No pressure, just guidance
- Match product to actual needs
- Honesty over upselling

## Voice & Tone Guidelines

### Brand Voice (Consistent Across All Content)

**Honest:**
- Say what you mean, mean what you say
- No exaggeration or hype
- Admit what you don't know
- If a product isn't right for someone, say so

**Helpful:**
- Explain, don't just sell
- Anticipate questions and answer them
- Provide context (why this matters)
- Guide toward right choice, not most expensive choice

**Local:**
- Mention Victoria, Vancouver Island naturally
- Reference local neighborhoods when relevant
- Show understanding of local home styles
- Support local community (contractors, businesses)

**Conversational:**
- Write like you talk (but edited)
- Use contractions (we're, it's, you'll)
- Address reader as "you"
- Occasional humor (but don't force it)

### Tone Variations (Situational)

**Deal Announcements:** Excited but not pushy
> "This week's deal is a good one—like, really good."

**Product Descriptions:** Informative and clear
> "The 0.55mm wear layer means this floor can handle high traffic without showing wear."

**Educational Content:** Patient and thorough
> "Let's break down what those soundproofing ratings actually mean for your home."

**Customer Service:** Empathetic and solution-focused
> "Let's figure out what happened and make it right."

**About Page:** Warm and trustworthy
> "We're a local Victoria business that happens to sell flooring—not a flooring corporation that happens to be in Victoria."

## Messaging Don'ts

❌ **Don't use:**
- Corporate jargon ("leverage," "solutions," "synergy")
- Fake urgency ("ACT NOW!!!" "ONLY 2 LEFT!!!")
- Overpromising ("life-changing," "revolutionary")
- Cold formality ("Please be advised that...")
- Passive voice ("Mistakes were made")
- All caps (except rare emphasis in headlines)

❌ **Don't:**
- Manipulate pricing ("was $9.99, now $4.99" when it was never $9.99)
- Pressure customers ("You'd be crazy not to buy this")
- Oversell ("This floor will change your life")
- Use stock photo copy ("Experience excellence in flooring solutions")
- Bury the point (get to the benefit quickly)

## A/B Testing Recommendations

### Email Subject Lines
**Test:** Benefit vs. Deal  
- A: "Quiet Floors That Handle Real Life" (benefit-focused)
- B: "14% Off Harbinger York LVP This Week" (deal-focused)

**Test:** Question vs. Statement  
- A: "Looking for Durable Flooring in Victoria?" (question)
- B: "Victoria's Best Flooring Deal This Week" (statement)

### Landing Page Headlines
**Test:** Feature vs. Benefit  
- A: "Cork Underlayment + 0.55mm Wear Layer" (feature)
- B: "Quiet Comfort Meets Lasting Durability" (benefit)

**Test:** Direct vs. Story  
- A: "Get This Floor for $4.29/sqft (14% Off)" (direct)
- B: "The Best Flooring Deal in Victoria? Decide for Yourself." (story)

### CTA Button Text
**Test:** Action vs. Value  
- A: "Get Your Free Sample" (action)
- B: "See It in Your Home—Free Sample" (value)

**Test:** Urgency vs. Benefit  
- A: "Order Today" (urgency)
- B: "Start Your Project" (benefit)

## Crisis Communication (If Needed)

### Product Recall or Quality Issue

**Immediate Response (Same Day):**
> "We've been notified of [issue] with [product]. If you purchased this product between [dates], please contact us immediately at [phone/email]. We're working directly with [manufacturer] to resolve this and will make it right for affected customers."

**Follow-Up (Within 48 Hours):**
> "Here's what we know: [details]. Here's what we're doing: [action plan]. Here's what you should do: [customer steps]. We'll update you as we learn more."

**Key Principles:**
- Acknowledge quickly (don't hide)
- Be specific about what you know and don't know
- Focus on customer safety/satisfaction
- Provide clear next steps
- Follow up consistently

### Service Failure

**Response:**
> "We fell short of our standards, and we're sorry. Here's what happened: [explanation]. Here's how we're fixing it: [solution]. Here's how we'll prevent it: [prevention]."

**Key Principles:**
- Own the mistake (no deflection)
- Explain what went wrong (briefly)
- Focus on solution
- Make it right for customer
- Show steps to prevent recurrence
