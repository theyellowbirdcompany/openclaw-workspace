The Deal at a Glance

The Harbinger Signature Acoustic Click in York is priced at $4.29 per square foot this week — that's 70 cents off the regular $4.99 price, or about 14% savings. For a 400 sqft main living area, that's $280 back in your pocket before you've even picked up a plank.

What Makes This Flooring Different: The Technical Case

Most LVP in the $3–$5 range comes with a 0.3mm or 0.4mm wear layer. The Harbinger York comes with a 0.55mm wear layer. That extra thickness matters more than it sounds.

A thicker wear layer means your floor can handle more years of foot traffic before showing wear. It's more resistant to scratching from furniture legs, pet nails, and everyday use. If you're putting this in a main living room, kitchen, or hallway — anywhere that actually gets used — 0.55mm is the right spec to look for.

Quick Tip

When comparing LVP products, always check the wear layer spec first. A floor at $3.50/sqft with a 0.3mm wear layer will cost more in the long run than a $4.29 floor with 0.55mm — especially in high-traffic areas.

The Sound Story: IIC 73 / STC 68

Here's where the Harbinger York really separates itself from average LVP. It ships with an integrated cork underlayment — not a thin foam layer, but actual cork. The result is an industry-leading acoustic rating:

  • IIC 73 — Impact Isolation Class (measures how much impact noise, like footsteps, transfers between floors)
  • STC 68 — Sound Transmission Class (measures airborne noise like voices and music)

For context: most building codes require IIC and STC ratings of 50 for multi-family residential. This floor scores 23 points above that minimum. If you live in a condo, have a basement suite, or simply want quieter rooms — those numbers mean something real.

What It Looks Like: The York Aesthetic

York is a light oak colourway with a realistic embossed grain texture. It reads as warm and natural without veering into overly rustic territory. It works well in open-plan living areas, main bedrooms, and home offices — anywhere you want light, clean, and livable.

The embossing is registered — meaning the texture aligns with the printed grain pattern. This is what gives it that tactile credibility when you run your hand across it. Cheaper LVP skips this step, and you can feel it.

Installation: What "Floating Click" Actually Means for You

The Harbinger Signature uses a floating click-lock system. The planks snap together without glue or nails, and the floor "floats" above the subfloor. This matters for two reasons:

  • DIY-friendly: Most homeowners with basic tools and a free weekend can install it themselves. No contractor required unless you want one.
  • Subfloor flexibility: Floating floors tolerate minor subfloor imperfections better than glue-down products. In older Victoria homes — where subfloors are rarely perfectly level — this reduces prep work.

The integrated cork underlayment also means you don't need to buy and lay a separate underlayment sheet first. One fewer step, one less material to source.

Who This Deal Is Built For

The Harbinger York is the right product for homeowners who are upgrading a main living area and want a floor that handles real life. That means:

  • Families with kids and pets who need durability without sacrificing looks
  • Condo owners or those with below-grade suites where sound transmission is a concern
  • Anyone replacing carpet in a main bedroom or living room and wants a quieter, warmer feel underfoot
  • DIYers who want a click-and-go installation without hiring trades

It's not the cheapest floor on the market. It's the floor that justifies its price with every spec.
